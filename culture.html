History of South Korea

Ancient History

South Korea, officially the Republic of Korea, has a long history and was ruled by kings for many centuries. Ancient kingdoms like Goguryeo, Baekje, and Silla, and later the Goryeo and Joseon dynasties, shaped its culture and traditions. Many of their palaces and historical sites still exist today, allowing people to see the country’s rich cultural past. After Japanese rule (1910–1945) and the Korean War (1950–1953), South Korea developed into a modern, democratic, and industrialized nation.

Celebrations & Festivals

Chuseok (Harvest Festival)

Chuseok is one of South Korea’s most important traditional festivals, celebrated to give thanks for a good harvest. Families gather to perform ancestral rites and share traditional foods.

Traditional Costumes

Korean traditional costumes, known as hanbok, have been worn for centuries during festivals and special occasions. Hanbok is designed to be elegant and comfortable, with bright colors and flowing shapes representing harmony and balance in Korean culture.
